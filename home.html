Syllabus & Curriculum
This section contains information about syllabus, curriculum, lecture PDFs, notes, books, solved quizzes & more.

  • Course Instructor: Phone No. 01881-24 2181
    Dr. M. Prabhakar Email: prabhakar@iitrpr.ac.in
    Pre-requisites: Basic Knowledge in Single Variable Calculus

  • Syllabus for Mid Semester Examination: Modules 1-3
    Module 1: Revision of basic concepts: Real Number system, Field Axioms, supremum, infimum, Archimedian property, finite, countable, and uncountable sets. (Approximately 4 lectures)
    Module 2: Metric spaces, completeness, connectedness, compactness, Heine-Borel theorem, totally bounded sets, finite intersection property, completeness of R^n, Banach fixed point theorem, perfect sets, the Cantor set. (Approximately 8 lectures)
    Module 3: Convergent Sequences, Subsequences, Cauchy Sequences, Series, the number e, power series, Continuous functions, relation with connectedness and compactness, discontinuity, uniform continuous functions, monotone functions, Fixed point theorem. (Approximately 9 lectures)

  • Syllabus for End Semester Examination : Modules 4-6
    Module 4: Revision on Differentiability and its properties, mean value theorem, Taylor's theorem. Riemann-Stieltjes integral with properties and characterization, improper integral, Rectifiable curves, total variation, and functions of bounded variations. (Approximately 9 lectures)
    Module 5: Sequence and series of functions, Uniform convergence, uniform convergence and its relation with continuity, differentiability, and integrability, Weierstrass approximation theorem, Equicontinuous family of functions. (Approximately 6-8 lectures)
    Module 6: Arzela-Ascoli theorem. Fejer's theorem, power series, and Fourier series. (Approximately 4-6 lectures)

    • Text Books/ Reference Books:
    • Walter Rudin, Principles of Mathematical Analysis, Third Edition, McGRAW-HILL, 1976.

    • C. C. Pugh, Real Mathematical Analysis, Springer, 2004.

    • T.M. Apostol, Mathematical Analysis , 2nd Edition, Narosa, 1985.

    • H. L. Royden, Real Analysis , 3rd Edition, Prentice Hall of India, 1995.

    Grading Scheme:
    Apart from Mid Semester and End Semester examinations, there will be a minimum of 6 Assignments (3 Assignments before Mid Semester Examination, 3 Assignments after Mid Semester Examination). The minimum pass mark for this course will be 30%.
    Marks Distribution:
    6 Assignments – 30 Marks
    Mid Semester – 35 Marks
    End Semester – 35 Marks

This course introduces the basics of data structures and algorithms. Topics span across basic data structures (e.g., arrays, stacks, linked lists, queues, heaps, etc.), algorithm analysis (e.g., asymptotic analysis, master theorem, etc.), search Trees (e.g., AVL Trees, redblack trees, etc.), sorting (e.g., quick sort, linear time sorting, order statistics, etc.), hashing (e.g., dictionaries, tries, etc.) and Graphs (e.g., graph data structures, graph traversal algorithms such as BFS and DFS, minimum spanning Tree, shortest path algorithms, etc.)
